ORIGINS OF NISSAN

The name of the great car manufacturer Nissan is originated in Japen when their very own founder Yoshisuke Aikawa became president of Nihon Sangya in early 1928 since than nissan expanded imports beyond its Japanese roots to worldwide and historically came to the U.S. as Datsun in late 1958. Initally this company was called "Zaibatsu" which also included other two companies know as Tobata Casting and Hitachi as they were doing well in the world wide.In 1931,DAT Jidosha started cooperating with Tobata Casting and eventually merged into Tobata Casting in 1933. THE ROAD FROM JAPAN TO AMERICA

Nissan manufactured their very first cars at the flagship Nishi-ku,Yokohama headquarters in 1935.Nissan showed its first Datsuns (a 1000 cc car and truck) at early 1959 Los Abgeles Auto Show.In order to remain competitive in global market,Nissan also started manufacturing in Mexico at 1966 and after years of hardwork they also started production in Australia and Taiwan and finally making first American factory opening at Smyrna in 1983.
